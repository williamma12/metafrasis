Μετάφρασις

Free, open-source OCR for Ancient Greek manuscripts. Extract text from printed books and handwritten documents with ease.

Download for macOS

Version 1.0.0 · macOS 10.15+ · Intel & Apple Silicon

Features

📜

Ancient Greek OCR

Specialized recognition for polytonic Greek text, including accents, breathing marks, and archaic characters.

✍️

Handwriting Support

Powered by trOCR transformer models for accurate recognition of handwritten manuscripts.

📄

PDF Support

Upload multi-page PDFs and process them automatically. Each page is converted and OCR'd.

🔍

Interactive Viewer

View recognized text with word-level bounding boxes. Click to toggle, hover for details.

⚡

GPU Accelerated

Automatic GPU detection for faster processing. Supports CUDA and Apple Silicon (MPS).

💻

Fully Offline

Everything runs locally on your machine. Your documents never leave your computer.

Installation

1

Download the DMG

Click the download button above to get the latest version of Metafrasis.

2

Open the DMG

Double-click the downloaded Metafrasis.dmg file to mount it.

3

Drag to Applications

Drag the Metafrasis app to your Applications folder.

4

Launch the App

Open Metafrasis from your Applications folder or Launchpad.

First Launch Note

Since the app isn't signed with an Apple Developer ID, macOS may show a warning. To open it: Right-click (or Control-click) the app and select "Open", then click "Open" in the dialog.

How to Use

1 Select an OCR Engine

Choose from the sidebar:

  • Tesseract – Fast, best for printed text
  • trOCR – Slower but excellent for handwritten manuscripts

2 Upload Your Files

Drag and drop or click to upload:

  • Images: PNG, JPG, JPEG
  • Documents: PDF (multi-page supported)

3 Run OCR

Click the "Run OCR" button. Processing time depends on your hardware and the selected engine. GPU acceleration is used automatically when available.

4 View Results

The interactive viewer shows recognized text overlaid on your image. Click words to toggle visibility, hover for confidence scores. Copy the extracted text or export your results.
